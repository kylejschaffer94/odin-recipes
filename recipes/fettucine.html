Fettucine Alfredo

Description

Fettuccine Alfredo is a decadent and indulgent pasta dish that hails from Italian-American cuisine. At its core, it consists of wide, ribbon-like fettuccine noodles bathed in a luxuriously creamy and buttery sauce. The sauce is created by combining heavy cream, butter, and grated Parmesan cheese, resulting in a velvety texture and a rich, savory flavor profile. The al dente fettuccine noodles are the perfect canvas for this luscious sauce, allowing it to cling to each strand, creating a harmonious and satisfying bite. The simplicity of the ingredients and the delicate balance between creaminess and cheesiness make Fettuccine Alfredo an Italian classic that has become beloved worldwide for its comforting and indulgent qualities.

Fettuccine Alfredo can be enjoyed on its own as a comforting main dish or paired with additions like grilled chicken, shrimp, or broccoli for added flavor and texture. Its creamy, cheesy goodness is the epitome of comfort food, making it a go-to choice for those seeking a satisfying and luxurious pasta experience. Whether served on a special occasion or as a delightful weeknight treat, Fettuccine Alfredo never fails to deliver a taste of creamy pasta perfection that captures the essence of Italian-American cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces fettuccine pasta
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish, optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the Fettuccine: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine pasta and cook according to the package instructions until it reaches al dente. Drain the pasta and set it aside.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add Cream and Parmesan: Pour in the heavy cream, and bring it to a gentle simmer while stirring continuously. Reduce the heat to low.
  4. Incorporate Parmesan Cheese: Gradually whisk in the grated Parmesan cheese into the simmering cream mixture. Continue to stir until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce thickens. This usually takes about 3-5 minutes.
  5. Season with Salt and Pepper: Taste the sauce and season with salt and black pepper to your liking. Remember that Parmesan cheese is already salty, so be cautious with the salt.
  6. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked and drained fettuccine pasta directly into the skillet with the Alfredo sauce. Gently toss the pasta until it's evenly coated with the creamy sauce.
  7. Serve: Plate the creamy Fettuccine Alfredo, garnishing it with chopped fresh parsley if desired. Serve immediately while it's hot, and enjoy the rich and indulgent flavors of this classic pasta dish.
